Welcome to the Rick and Morty Universe

Join the misadventures of Rick Sanchez and his grandson Morty as they travel across dimensions, encounter strange beings, and navigate the chaos of the multiverse.

About the Show

Rick and Morty is an animated sci-fi sitcom following the exploits of an eccentric, alcoholic scientist and his easily distressed grandson. The show explores themes of existentialism, family dysfunction, and the bizarre nature of alternate realities.

Rick and Morty Show Summary

This show follows the misadventures of an alcoholic scientist Rick and his nervous grandson Morty. If they aren't home with their family causing mayhem, then they are on one of their many intergalactic travel adventures doing so.

Understanding the Rick and Morty Universe

The world that these two characters occupy is a part of a multiverse. This means there are an infinite number of universes with copies of themselves that exist in time, all of whom are different. However, in some way this duo and all that exist of them seem to be the center of everything as seen through the creation of the Citadel. However, along the way they bump into many other characters that aren't themselves.

Main Characters

Rick Sanchez

A brilliant but boozy scientist who takes his grandson Morty on interdimensional adventures.

Morty Smith

Rick's grandson, he is in high school and whilst trying to pass his classes (especially maths with Mr. Goldenfold) Rick is always taking him out of class to help him out on his adventures.

Beth Sanchez

Rick's daughter, he left her and his wife when she was a child and only came back 20 years later. Beth has abandonment issues due to this and she is terrified of her father leaving again. She is the father of Morty and Summer and the wife of Jerry.

Jerry Smith

The very scared husband of Beth, father of Morty and Summer, he is a very timid guy and is scared of almost everything. He doesn't appreciate Rick after leaving Beth and for taking Morty on “high-tech sci-fi-rigmarole”.

Summer Smith

Morty's sister and the daughter of Beth and Jerry, she always tries to prove herself as a good companion to Rick so she'll be invited more but is actually quite an emotional teenager at heart. She cried when the Devil fired her from the business she helped him create.

This is only the show's main characters and in an interdimensional reality as crazy and wild as Rick and Morty, it can be hard to keep all of the characters straight.
